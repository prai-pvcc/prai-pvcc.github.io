Chapter 12: Models of Computation

  1. What is the purpose of a model?
    2. A model, in general terms, serves as a simplified representation of a complex system, process, or phenomenon. It is an abstract or conceptual framework that helps us understand, analyze, and predict the behavior of the subject being modeled.
  2. List four phenomena of a model:
    3. A.It captures the essence, the important properties of the real thing.
    B.Probably differs in scale from the real thing.
    C.Omits some of the details of the real thing.
    D.Lacks the full functionality of the real thing
  3. List four things a computing agent should be able to do:
    4. A.Accept input
    B.Store information in memory and retrieve it from memory
    C.Take actions according to algorithm instructions
    D.Produce output
  4. List four characteristics of an algorithm:
    5. A.Be a well-ordered collection
    B.Consist of unambiguous and effectively computable operations
    C.Halt in a finite amount of time
    D.Produce a result
  5. What is the "halting problem" with regards to an uncomputable problem?
    6. The halting problem is a fundamental result in computability theory, as it demonstrates that there are problems that cannot be solved by algorithms or computation, no matter how powerful the computing machinery.

Chapter 13:Simulation & Modeling

Define these terms:
  1. Scientific Method:
    2. The scientific method is a systematic process for acquiring knowledge and understanding the natural world. It involves the formulation of hypotheses, careful observation, experimentation, and analysis of data to draw conclusions and refine our understanding.
  2. Computional model
    3. A computational model is a mathematical or algorithmic representation of a system, process, or phenomenon that can be simulated or analyzed using computers.
  3. discrete event Simulation
    4. Discrete event simulation (DES) is a modeling and simulation technique used to study systems or processes where events occur at specific points in time, causing changes in the state of the system.
  4. List and describe seven reasons to construct a simulation
    1. Existence:The system might not yet exist, so it is not possible to experiment directly on the actual system. In fact, we might be using a model to help us with the construction of the system.
    2. Physical realization:The system is not constructed from entities that can be represented by physical objects. For example, it may be a social system (such as welfare policies or labor practices) that can only be simulated on a computer.
    3. Speed of Construction:It might take too much time to construct a physical model.
    4. Time Scale:Some physical systems change too slowly or too quickly. For example, an elementary particle in a high-speed accelerator may decompose in 10215 seconds.
    5. Ethical Behavior:Some physical models have serious moral and ethi- cal consequences, perhaps the best known being the use of animals in medical research.
    6. Ease of Modification:If we are not happy with the design of a physical model, we need to construct a brand-new one.
    7. Safety:It might be too dangerous to experiment on the actual system or a physical replica.

Illuestires: "the Mother of All computations":
  1. What is Illustris:
    2. The model, called Illustris, tracked the evolution of the cosmos from a few hundred thousand years after the Big Bang up to the current time, 13.8 billion years later.
  2. What fields of science were involved in the Illustris computational model?
    3. Illustris uses equations drawn from physics, astronomy, hydrodynamics, chemistry, and mathematics.
  3. How long did the project take?
    4. It took five years for the software teams in the United States, France, and Germany to design, program, and test Illustris.
  4. >How many years of gallactic evolution are simulated in Illustris?
    5. 14 billion years.
  5. Illustris was created using two supercomputers. What is a supercomputer?
    6. A supercomputer is a computer with a high level of performance as compared to a general-purpose computer.
